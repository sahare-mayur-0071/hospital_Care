Emergency First Aid Guidelines

Cardiac Emergency

  • Call emergency services immediately (911)
  • If trained, begin CPR if the person is unresponsive and not breathing normally
  • Push hard and fast in the center of the chest at a rate of 100-120 compressions per minute
  • If an AED is available, use it according to instructions
  • Continue CPR until emergency services arrive

Stroke

  • Remember the acronym FAST:
    • Face: Ask the person to smile. Does one side of the face droop?
    • Arms: Ask the person to raise both arms. Does one arm drift downward?
    • Speech: Ask the person to repeat a simple phrase. Is speech slurred or strange?
    • Time: If you observe any of these signs, call 911 immediately
  • Note the time when symptoms first appeared
  • Do not give them medication, food, or drink

Severe Bleeding

  • Apply direct pressure to the wound using a clean cloth or bandage
  • If blood soaks through, add more material without removing the original cloth
  • If possible, elevate the wounded area above the heart
  • Apply pressure to the arterial pressure point if necessary
  • Secure the dressing with a bandage when bleeding slows
  • Seek immediate medical attention
